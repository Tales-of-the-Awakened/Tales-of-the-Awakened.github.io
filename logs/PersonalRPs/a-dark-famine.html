[UNKNOWN DATE, THE OLD WAR] OPEN RP - Ahuna Mons Valley, Ceres ~The blue skies of Ceres were unrecognizable under the darkness of smoke. Fires raged along the pristine shorelines, the acidic lakes bubbling beyond as it attempted to dissolve the wreckage of skips and monstrous machines alike. Bullets and lasers pierced the air, scorching metal armour and sizzling as flesh was seared. Along the shore, a squad of warriors, Dax, clad in bronze armour, fired their weapons down the sandy shores as a wave of vicious, undulating forms writhed across the sand. Their weapons rattled as they expelled bullets at the encroaching beasts, their zero-tech armour reflecting both the faint rays of light that shone through the smoke, as well as the brilliant blue beams that aimed to disintegrate them. Every so often, a stray ray would strike one of the warriors on the flesh- their hand, perhaps, or their exposed mouths- and soon enough, their body was nothing more than a spray of red mist. The squad of 8- no, 5 left now, stepped backwards, continually retreating as their enemy tried to weather their gunfire. The Sentients. Their forms were abstract, almost shapeless, not yet fully decided as to what form would be best suited for this mission. But that didn't stop them. The Dax's bullets that grazed the red shells of the vile machines only caused them to strengthen, visibly causing the formless masses to gain some shape for a moment, if not permanently. They adapted slowly, constantly, as the bullets continued to pelt them. But it wasn't in vain. The projectiles striking the Sentients often hit exposed internal organs, not yet adapted to take such damage, causing the owner of such a flaw to fall to pieces, quickly trampled by its brethren behind.~ Dax 1: "Voiddamnit where's everyone else?" One of the Dax screamed over the unholy chorus of gunfire and Sentient song, "We had at least thirty men!"
Dax 2: "Sir, it was that big one! It wiped out nearly the entire platoon in one strike! We're not equipped for this!" The Dax closest to the first tossed his Braton Prime aside, its magazine empty and his own pockets devoid of ammo. He pulled out a Lato and began to unload the weapon all the same. Dax 1: "You bet your kubrow-eating rump we aren't equipped for this! By their holiness, this is Voiddamn Tenno work, I tell you!" The mere 2 second decrease in bullets was enough to let one of the Sentients catch up with them. It leapt forward, its 'head' splitting open into a myriad of horrifying, bladed tendrils. They hooked the first Dax, scooping it up into its face before expelling the gore outside of its hollow chest. THE TREES!" The second Dax dropped his Lato and sprinted sideways, perpendicular to the other Dax's path. He headed straight for the trees, away from the shore. Dax 4: "The evac zone is further down the beach! They'll never find us..." but it was too late. The fifth and final Dax warrior followed after the second, not wasting a single breath on words. With a grunt, the fourth Dax rolled to the side, narrowly dodging the Sentients' far more intense output of fire now that the humans had halted their own return. The Sentients created a mighty crashing sound as their seemingly endless swarm hit the treeline, but it slowed them down enough for the few surviving Dax to escape.
~Days passed. The Dax warriors hacked through the jungle, their Nikanas dulling on the tree branches that barred their way. But it wasn't in vain. Soon enough, they found their way to a clearing, and caught the attention of a passing Orokin gunship. The three Dax exfiltrated quickly, not wasting a single second- for how far behind could the Sentients truly be? More days passed, and the Dax survivors returned to the Orokin planetside fortress- Udanta, the base of operations for the Ceres campaign. It had been forty seven days since initial Sentient contact here, and things had only recently taken a turn for the worse. Tenno involvement had been considered unnecessary due to the seemingly weak numbers of Sentients here, but it was a grave mistake- the Sentients appeared to have erected some kind of replication facility to churn out massive amounts of their own kind, sucking out the raw minerals on Ceres in the process. Now, Sentient Brood Ships had arrived, guarding the facility from orbital bombardment, and delivering even more reinforcements. The Dax forces were being pushed back, and the three survivors' tale was just one of the many ill news delivered to the commander stationed at Udanta. Commander Tamaska. He was no war hero- he was a student of war, as many of the Dax were. But these Sentients followed no tactics ever recorded in human warfare. The Executors Tamaska had to report to were so far sated by his lies of success, but it was only a matter of time before the Sentients were on Udanta's doorstep. And that is why he is requesting Tenno aid.

If any Sentients were watching the skies, which knowing them they were, they'd see the two of them coming from miles off. "Aurelia?" Muro called over the squad comms. "Did that General give you and Ide any idea on what we should be expecting? Maybe a quick and quiet way in?"
